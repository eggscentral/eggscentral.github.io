LOCATIONS

EGGS LOCATIONS

Headquarters. If you don't know what that is, use a dictionary.


    Like the Pentagon, but better. This is where the main offices of EGGS are. In an undisclosed location, the most senior members of EGGS hierarchy file paperwork, coordinate missions, relay communications, think up mind-bending plans, etc. Director Robert Michaels, Vice Director Lily Warden and the five heads of departments all rub elbows here. The walls lean outwards, making it just that tiny bit harder for TASTY agents to scale, if they ever find the building in the first place. 



Archdirector Robert Michaels' Office

    

    This is one of the most highly secured places in the Hexagon. It has all the security measures you would expect from a spy organization, and then some. With it's back to the door, a cozy white desk, and a fake-leather swivelly chair stands behind it. It's high black neck rest seems to preside over the rest of the room, and if you lean in and take a deep breath you can catch a whiff of hair gel. No matter it's unassuming appearance, if need be it can travel up to 80 kph, powered by a small engine the Science division devised. Fantastic way to win office chair races! Behind it, a one-way glass forms the western wall, offering a breath-taking view of the sunset. 



The Halo-The helipad. The letter H wore off, leaving a bright yellow circle.


    Situated on the Hexagon roof, this is where agents meet to leave for far-away assignments. At night, it's illuminated by landing-strip lights. Every year at the EGGS Sports Day, teams fly a kilometer up into the air and have three hours to design a parachute that allows an egg to touch down unscathed with only newspaper, tape, string, pipe cleaners, a plastic bag and straws. The Science team has a twenty-three year winning streak. Losing teams have to clean the eggs up, and raw eggs are used. 



Lamp Lady's Lair-A tribute to Florence Nightingale. 


    Where injured agents come to heal. The "lair" is actually the entire Block C, boasting five floors, one for the operating theatres, two for wards, one for offices and another for physical therapy. Each ward is named for a different famous nurse, making it a bit confusing for people who don't know their history to navigate-they're in chronological order. Behind the reception desk is a framed portrait of Florence Nightingale, watching over all who enter. Agent Luke Cone holds the record for the most visits to date, with 394 trips in the bag. 



The Lion's Den-Vice Director Lily Warden's office. She has a tendency to bite the head off idiots.


    For convenience's sake, the door is twice the width of a regular one. The floor is astroturf, lined with ruts from the weight of a wheelchair. A mahogany desk ornamented with coffee mug stains, minus a chair poises on a heavy woven carpet, with an alarm button concealed on the underside. Her window wall, unlike the Director's, faces east. As opposite as people can be, they are each other's yin and yang. Many junior agents have placed bets on when they will get together. She likes to wake early and sleep early, while he is not a morning person and does paperwork until late at night, like their office windows. 



The Wall of Honour


    Walking through the glass entry doors of the Hexagon, this is the first thing you see. A gleaming wall with engraved bronze plaques, bearing the names who have fallen in combat. The very first one is ANTHEA NICOLE COULTAIN-ROGERS 1955. Among them are JÃ„GER DOVE 1980, MARIAN MAYHAND 1991, and DART FOSTER 2013. Every year on November 11, agents everywhere stop and take a minute to remember those who sacrificed their lives for EGGS. 



The Wishing Well-Ahh, so serene.


    First of all, it's not actually a well. It's actually a fountain, but the name stuck after training one day when a greenie was so thirsty he drank from the decorative fountain instead of the designated water fountain twenty metres away. The wishing part comes from when on August 19, the day when fourth-year trainees begin their first day of work, started to throw coins in wishing to do well and not muck up. At the end of each month, the coins are collected and put into the EGGS funds. Once, a whopping $6,873 was counted. The Wishing Well is a favourite lunch spot.


TASTY LOCATIONS

This is what we, EGGS, have managed to piece together from intel gathered from spies and TASTY agents.

Alcatraz-Their interrogation/prison facilities.
    We don't have a lot of intel on this. We discovered it by accident when Hatch was taken prisoner and we tracked her there, and she doesn't want to speak about it anytime soon.

Brainwashers-Where they train the new recruits. They call it the Institute.
    They have barracks at KG5, German Swiss International, an unknown Woodlands preschool and at Glenealy. There may be more, but we don't have confirmation. Glenealy is the biggest barracks, as it's built on a humongous mountain-lots of earth to tunnel under! 

The Factory Farms-Where did you think they got their meat?
    Think of a factory farm. Now multiply the stink, noise, filth, crowd and all-round terror and you have your basic TASTY farm. They are strategically placed under schools in the countryside, with less pipelines and wires running underground. Tiny fist-sized holes allow the bare minimum of vitamin D to be produced. The life expectancy for animals in one of these factories is 40% lower than the average.

Fund-raising Stations-Where they get their funds.
    Every. Single. Fast. Food. Shop. In. The. World.

Headquarters-Every organization has one.
    Every TASTY agent has rather died than give this away, but we have one clue: the last words forced out of an agent were "It's at S-" and then anti-climatically, he died.
